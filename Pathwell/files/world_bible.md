## THE BLOBS

**What they are:** Cleanup crews. They consume **unclaimed spent potential** — spillage. The energy that escaped a transaction and was never ledgered.

**What this explains:**
- The Space Between and the camp archive are not buffets, because their charge is *claimed/stored*, not spilled.
- Pathwell pruned for a very long time without seeing blobs — ledgered transactions don't summon them.
- Shade is the greatest spillage in the world: a prune that escaped the ledger and took on flesh. Shade's existence is a standing dinner bell.
- The blobs pursue Pathwell *and* Shade because they cannot distinguish source from spill — the signatures are nearly identical.

**Arrival is not instant.** Blobs need time to sense a spike in spillage and time to cross into this dimension. A signature event starts a clock; the blobs arrive *after a delay*. [Whether a specific prune triggers a specific later arrival is scene-level and tracked in the conflicts doc where relevant.]

**Sensory signature:** stench of wet-rot / low tide. Amorphous, at least six feet. Dark ooze. The eye wants to slide off them.

**Targeting:** They go for spillage. At the climax they go for Elizabeth because she is immobile and vulnerable in a room that already has reason to be full of them (two near-identical signatures present) — NOT because they ever "tasted" or marked her. The "blobs track Elizabeth after the bar" rule is CUT; do not reintroduce it.

## CASTING RESIDUE (Chapter 1 mechanism)

A combat casting is not a ledgered transaction — the charge is released, consumed, scattered, and residue clings to whatever/whoever it was cast on. Fresh residue is, briefly, real spillage.

This is what drives the Chapter 1 misread: Pathwell burns a page of the cookbook to ward Elizabeth; the residue clings to her; a blob already in the room presses toward the freshest spillage — her — passing over Pathwell. He reads the blob's turn as evidence *she* is significant. He is wrong. It is his own casting on her skin. (Full misread chain in character_bible.md under Pathwell.)

Same rule, later appearances: the bar mirror flares a fresh signature (the blob attack is its cost); the climactic prune *tears* (a catastrophic fresh spill). One rule, multiple payoffs, never explained on the page.

## THE SPACE BETWEEN

A shop/world reached through a threshold. Run by a shopkeeper figure (see glossary for the locked name). Charges for what it provides; payment in rare/exotic forms and in potential. Archives and shops in this world operate on a shared principle:

**The archive rule:** *What is given to an archive becomes part of it. Withdrawals cost.* Consistent with the Space Between's shopkeeper charging for transactions. This rule must be planted in Act II (one line when Elizabeth entrusts her diary to the camp archive) so the Act III diary-retrieval price is grounded rather than arbitrary.
